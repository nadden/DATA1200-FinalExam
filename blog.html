LIFE AT OSLOMET

There has not been a single day where I did not want to design and be able to create things. I've always knew that I would go to University, but to find out what I wanted to study has been one of the biggest challenges. I've always wanted to learn to code, but it seemed so difficult and I've been afarid for that this may not be something for me anyway.

TRANSITION AND STUDENT LIFE

The transition from being a pupil to a student was overwhelming. It was not easy for me to adjust to the new lifestyle. Applied data science was also way outside my comfort zone and I did not have much experience with coding, which led to stress and many negative thoughts about if I was capable to get through my courses. I wondered how I would handle self-study and minimum physical lectures and whether I had enough discipline and smart enough to be able to read and study without having weekly assignments and tests. In addition to moving to a new city where I do not know so many and making new friends.

The two sections below I will take you through how I experienced moving and adapting to a new city, and finding a study technique that suited me.

ADJUSTING TO A NEW CITY

Not everyone chooses or needs to move away from home when they are going to college. I am from a small municipality in Hedmark and did not have much choice. STUDY TECHNIQUES

Finding the right study technique is essential and it can save you a lot of time when studying to different courses. On the other hand, it was difficult for me to find the right study technique right away, since the University was something completely different from what I was used to in High School, and I used a lot of time just to keeping up with the schedule and knowing when I had the different courses.

There is no final decision on which study technique to use, as it is very individual. It is therefore important to try at the beginning and not panic if you see that it does not work during the first semester, as this is very normal among students who have just gone from high school to university.

For me, it has worked very well to spend a lot of time on the resources that OsloMet uses and get to know it, so I know where I can find information, contact people if I need it etc. Also plan my week, keep up with lectures, syllabus and assignments has been essential for me.
